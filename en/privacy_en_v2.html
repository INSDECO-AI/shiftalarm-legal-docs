1. General data-processing principle

ShiftAlarm primarily follows an offline-first model. Core user data is created, processed and stored locally on the user's device. The developer does not receive such data automatically unless the user sends it through a support request, export, email, messenger, cloud storage, app store or another method selected by the user.

As of this version, the app does not use advertising networks, its own account system, server-side authorization, Firebase Analytics, Google Analytics, Crashlytics or AdMob unless this is expressly stated in an updated version of this Policy.

2. Data that may be stored locally

Depending on the features used, the app may store the following data on the device:

  • app settings, language, theme, widget settings and user preferences;
  • regular alarms, shift-linked alarms, time, repeats, sounds, vibration, snooze, fade-in and related settings;
  • shift schedules, shift templates, calendar overrides, days off and user marks;
  • geo-alarms: coordinates, radius, enter/exit event type, address, place name, saved places, schedule and trigger state;
  • notes, events, reminders, checklists, text blocks and related dates;
  • audio notes, voice recordings and text transcripts;
  • timer, stopwatch, world clocks and history of certain actions where such history is part of the feature;
  • salary calculation settings, bonuses, deductions, penalties, reports and related calculations;
  • diagnostic logs, technical events, permission status, errors and feature states;
  • backups if the user creates them manually.

3. Data the user may send to the developer

If the user contacts support at support@insdeco.ru, the developer may receive the user's email address, name or email signature, message text, attachments, screenshots, backup files, diagnostic reports, device model, Android version, app version, event time and issue description.

Such data is sent only by user action. The user should review the email and attachments before sending them.

4. Purposes of processing

Data is used to operate app features, save user settings, deliver alarms and reminders, operate geo-alarms, display notes and widgets, create backups, diagnose errors, process support requests, improve app stability, comply with legal requirements and protect the rights of the developer and the user.

Depending on applicable law, legal bases for processing may include performance of the user agreement, user action, consent for specific permissions, legitimate interest in support and security, and compliance with legal obligations.

5. Device permissions

The app may request only the permissions required for the relevant features:

  • notifications — for alarms, timers, reminders and service notifications;
  • exact alarms and alarm permissions — to trigger at the time selected by the user;
  • location while using the app — for selecting places, maps and setting up geo-alarms;
  • background location — for geo-alarms when the app is not open;
  • microphone — for audio notes, voice input and speech recognition;
  • internet — for downloading language packs, legal documents, help, instructions, offline transcription models, maps and network features;
  • file access through the system file picker — for backup export and import;
  • battery and background work settings — to improve reliability of alarms, notifications and geo-alarms.

The user can revoke permissions in Android settings. After revocation, some features may become unavailable or work with limitations.

6. Location and maps

Location data is used to select a point on a map, save geo-alarms, detect entering or leaving a defined area, display an address and create saved places. Coordinates and geo-alarm settings are stored locally on the device unless the user exports or sends them.

When the user uses maps or address lookup, the app may contact mapping and geocoding components, including osmdroid/OpenStreetMap, Nominatim reverse geocoding, the system Android Geocoder or another service available on the device. Such services may receive technical request data, IP address, coordinates or other data required to display the map or determine an address.

7. Audio, offline and online transcription

Audio notes and transcripts are created by user action and may contain personal or sensitive information if the user records it. They are stored locally on the device unless the user exports or sends them.

Offline transcription may use locally downloaded speech recognition models. When such models are downloaded, the remote server may see technical connection data such as IP address and request information.

Online speech recognition may use the Android system speech recognition service selected by the device, manufacturer, user or operating system. In this case, audio data and technical information may be sent to and processed by the selected speech recognition service under its own rules. The ShiftAlarm developer does not control the policies of that system service.

8. Network downloads and remote sources

The app may use the internet to download language packs, manifest files, legal documents, help materials, instructions and offline transcription models, including from GitHub or another remote source specified in the app. During such requests, the remote server may receive the IP address, date and time of the request, file path, connection information and other standard technical data.

Files for which integrity verification is implemented in the app may be checked using SHA-256 or another checksum. This reduces the risk of file corruption but does not guarantee remote source availability.

9. App stores and payments

The app is purchased through an app store, such as RuStore or Google Play, if the app is available on the relevant platform. The app store independently processes the user's account, payments, refunds, purchase history, installation and updates under its own rules and privacy policy.

The developer does not receive bank cards, payment passwords or full payment credentials when payment is made through the app store.

10. Backups and export

Backups are created only by user action. A backup file may contain a significant part of local app data, including alarms, schedules, notes, geo-points, settings, diagnostics and other information.

If the user saves or shares a backup through another app, cloud storage, email, messenger, file manager or external storage, further protection and processing of that file depends on the method of storage or transfer selected by the user.

11. Diagnostics

Diagnostic logs are intended for troubleshooting and feature checks. They may contain technical information about app events, time, permissions, device, OS version, app version, alarm state, geo-alarm state, notes, backups and errors.

Diagnostics are stored locally until the user clears them or manually sends them to the developer.

12. Sharing with third parties

The developer does not sell user data to advertisers and does not automatically transfer local app data to third parties. Data may be transferred if the user uses external services, sends a support request, exports a backup, uses maps, geocoding, online speech recognition, an app store or another external service.

Third-party services process data under their own rules. The user is encouraged to review the policies of the selected app store, email service, cloud storage, mapping service, speech recognition service and other external services.

13. Data retention

Local data is stored on the device until the user deletes it in the app, clears app data, uninstalls the app, restores data from another backup or deletes the backup file from the selected storage location.

Support requests, emails, attachments and diagnostic materials sent by the user to the developer may be stored without a predefined expiry period to preserve support history, analyze recurring issues, protect rights and comply with legal obligations. The user may request deletion of such data; the request will be fulfilled unless further retention is required or permitted by applicable law.

14. Security

The app uses standard Android mechanisms, including app data isolation by the operating system, system permissions and the system file picker. Certain downloaded files may be verified by checksum if such verification is implemented for that file type.

The user is responsible for protecting the device, screen lock, email access, cloud services, backups and external storage. Backups and exported files may be accessible to other people if the user stores them in an unprotected location or shares them with third parties.

15. Countries of processing

Local data remains on the user's device in the country where the device is used. Data transferred through app stores, GitHub, mapping services, speech recognition services, email services, cloud services and other external services may be processed in countries where those services operate or host infrastructure.

Data sent to developer support may be processed where the developer and the developer's email, hosting or backup services are located.

16. User rights

The user can view, change and delete most data directly in the app. The user can also clear app data through Android, uninstall the app, revoke permissions, export a backup or delete backup files created by the user.

For data sent to the developer, the user may contact support@insdeco.ru and request access, correction, deletion, restriction of processing or withdrawal of consent where such rights apply by law. To protect data, the developer may request information needed to verify that the request relates to the relevant user or email address.

If the user is located in a jurisdiction that provides a right to complain to a data protection authority, the user may contact such authority under applicable law.

17. Children

The app may have an age rating of 0+, but it is not intended for targeted collection of children's personal data by the developer. If a child uses the app, a parent or legal guardian should supervise settings, permissions, notes, audio recordings, geo-points, backups and support requests where required by law or family rules.

18. Changes to this policy

The developer may update this Policy. The current version may be delivered with the app, downloaded from a remote source or opened from a bundled fallback version. For material changes, the app may request that the user review or accept the updated document if required by law or app logic.
